# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

This project should be a practical, respectful place to build software. Contributors are expected to communicate with care, assume good intent while still addressing problems directly, and make room for people with different backgrounds, seniority levels, and working styles.

Examples of welcome behavior:

- Giving clear, specific feedback focused on the work.
- Asking questions before making personal assumptions.
- Sharing context that helps others contribute safely.
- Respecting boundaries around time, identity, privacy, and attribution.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, threats, insults, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Repeatedly derailing technical discussions with personal attacks.
- Misusing access to data, credentials, systems, or contributor information.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove comments, reject contributions, revoke access, or escalate issues when behavior harms contributors or users. Enforcement should be proportionate, documented when possible, and focused on protecting the community and the project.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns privately to a maintainer or repository owner. If the issue involves a maintainer, use another trusted project owner or the hosting platform's private reporting channel.

Reports should include what happened, when it happened, where it happened, and any relevant links or screenshots. Maintainers should acknowledge reports promptly and avoid sharing details beyond the people needed to resolve the issue.
